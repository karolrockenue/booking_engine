01

Toggle list.

Quiet, scannable. One row per add-on with a sliding ink toggle. This is what currently ships on the Portico booking page. Best when extras are a flat list and the room itself is the visual anchor above.

02

Card grid.

Editorial three-column grid. Each add-on gets its own image — same treatment as the rooms above the panel. Best when extras are visually distinctive (food, experiences) and the property has photography for them. Heavy on real estate.

03

Quick chips.

Horizontal chip selection — fast, low-commitment. Perfect when extras are short-named and don't need long descriptions to sell themselves. Frees up vertical space; the room blocks remain the page's focus.

04

Quantity steppers.

Each row carries a 0/1/2/3+ stepper instead of a binary toggle. Right when extras are quantity-aware (number of breakfasts, bottles, transfers) — turns "are these worth the bother of two flows?" into one flow. Most flexible; slightly heavier visually.

05

Editorial split.

Cinematic image left, scrollable add-on list right with thumbnails. Treats extras as a curated experience the same way the homepage hero treats the property. Strong brand statement; least space-efficient. Works on desktop, stacks on mobile.
