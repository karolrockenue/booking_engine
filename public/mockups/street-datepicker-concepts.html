Street · Ivory — date picker concepts

Five ways the check-in / check-out picker could open on the Street · Ivory home. All five live in the same cream-paper chrome (Fraunces serif numbers, hairline rules, ink fills for selected dates, single gold italic accent). They differ in form factor: classic two-month popover, single sliding month, inline expansion in-page, vertical-scroll (mobile-first), and side-panel. Same placeholder hotel and same selection (Thu 28 — Sat 30 May 2026, 2 nights) across all five.

/street-datepicker-concepts.html · 2026-05-27
D1

Two-month dropdown

The expected pattern. Popover drops below the active field, two months side-by-side. Range selection. Hairline rules, no chrome heaviness. Most familiar to budget-hotel bookers — Premier Inn, Booking.com do this.

D2

Single month · month strip on top

One month at a time. A horizontal strip of upcoming months sits above the grid — tap a month to jump straight to it. Bigger, more breathable day cells. Less expected, more elegant. Good if most stays are within the next 90 days.

D3

Inline expansion · no popover

No floating element. The search bar stays in place, a hairline appears below it, and the two months expand directly into the cream space below. No shadow, no border. Most native to Street's chrome — feels like part of the page, not a UI overlay.

D4

Vertical scroll · mobile-first

Airbnb's pattern. A narrow column with months stacked vertically, scroll to look further out. The same component works perfectly on phone (where most budget bookers are). Less efficient on desktop — only one month visible at a time and you scroll, not arrow.

Editorial side panel

D6

Combined "Dates" field · click twice to set range

One Dates field instead of two. Click → two-month dropdown opens. First click sets check-in, second click sets check-out (with hover preview of the range as you move). The Booking.com / Airbnb pattern. Less UI, larger cells, faster to use — matches muscle memory.
